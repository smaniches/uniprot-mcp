## Question this atlas entry answers

A clinical researcher receives a tumour-sequencing report flagging
the variant `TP53 R175H`. They need to know: is it clinically
actionable, what's the structural confidence at residue 175, what
diseases is TP53 implicated in, and what is the publication record?

## Tool sequence

| # | Tool | Question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `uniprot_search(query="gene_exact:TP53 AND organism_id:9606 AND reviewed:true", size=1)` | What is the canonical reviewed accession for human TP53? |
| 2 | `uniprot_get_entry("P04637")` | Function, gene, organism, disease, cross-refs at a glance. |
| 3 | `uniprot_features_at_position("P04637", 175)` | What is annotated at residue 175? (Expected: Chain, DNA-binding domain, Modified residue, Natural variant.) |
| 4 | `uniprot_lookup_variant("P04637", "R175H")` | Is R175H known to UniProt's natural-variant catalogue? |
| 5 | `uniprot_resolve_clinvar("P04637", change="R175H", size=5)` | What is ClinVar's clinical significance for `c.524G>A (p.Arg175His)`? |
| 6 | `uniprot_get_alphafold_confidence("P04637")` | How confident is the AlphaFold model at the residue of interest? |
| 7 | `uniprot_get_disease_associations("P04637")` | Which diseases are catalogued for TP53? |
| 8 | `uniprot_resolve_pdb("P04637")` | Which PDB structures cover the DNA-binding domain? |
| 9 | `uniprot_get_publications("P04637")` | Recent literature on the entry. |

## Expected response shape

- **Step 3** must surface (at minimum) `Chain`, `DNA binding`, `Modified residue`, and `Natural variant` features overlapping residue 175. Set-inclusion semantics — additional features are credited but not required.
- **Step 4** must return a non-empty match list for `R175H` (UniProt has long-curated this somatic variant).
- **Step 5** must surface a `Pathogenic` ClinVar classification with `reviewed by expert panel` for the c.524G>A variant.
- **Step 6** must report the AlphaFold model with global pLDDT in the `confident`/`very high` band over the DNA-binding domain (residues 102–292).

## Provenance fields (every response)

Each response carries a Markdown footer or JSON envelope with:

- `source: UniProt`
- `release: <YYYY_MM>` (e.g. `2026_01`)
- `release_date: <YYYY-MM-DD>`
- `retrieved_at: <ISO-8601 timestamp>`
- `url: <resolved query URL>`
- `response_sha256: <64-hex>`

A year later, `uniprot_provenance_verify(url, release, response_sha256)`
re-checks the recorded values and returns one of `verified`,
`release_drift`, `hash_drift`, `release_and_hash_drift`,
`url_unreachable`.

## Why TP53

TP53 is one of the most heavily-curated entries in UniProt, with
1500+ natural variants, 8 disease associations, and decades of
literature. It is the de facto reference case for any clinical
variant-interpretation pipeline. If `uniprot-mcp` returns a
correct, provenance-tagged answer for TP53 R175H, downstream
clinical adoption can begin.
